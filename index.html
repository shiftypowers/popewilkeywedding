More Details

What will the Sunday wedding reception entail?

We will have transportation to and from the venue from the Courtyard, the ceremony will happen at 4:30 pm followed by a cocktail hour that flows into the dinner reception, and a dance party (we are most excited for this!). Beverages (alcoholic and non-alcoholic) and food will be provided by Eleven Courses catering. We will have appetizers, entree stations, salads, and ice cream.

The event is semi-outside and may be partially subject to the natural elements, but will go on rain or shine. The venue does have an indoor section, where dinner will be based, and where shelter from the elements can be had throughout the night, if the storm necessitates it.

Will I be able to use Uber and Lyft to get around?

Ubers and Lyfts are plentiful in downtown and around the city.

What should I wear?

We encourage you to wear what makes you happiest and most comfortable. We have called the attire "Maui No Ka Oi" where floral patterns and aloha shirts (put them under a suit jacket!) are very welcome. For the ceremony on Sunday, please keep in mind the weather. There is a chance for rain or cold. Feel free to reach out if you have any questions!

What about mobility?

Our amazing planning team at the other side creatives will ensure everyone knows where to be at each point. If you will need assistance, please let us know so we can help coordinate a car. At all event locations, there will be wheelchair accessible restrooms.

Things To Do in Baltimore

The Walters Art Museum

There is a great art museum located in Mount Vernon-Belvedere downtown. It is a free public art museum where the collection was ammasssed by a father and son duo. The plaza and the building are both beautiful.Find the website here.

Baltimore Aquarium

AKA the National Aquarium, is the largest tourist attraction in Maryland. It houses several exhibits that cindlue a tropical rain forest, a multi-story coral reef, an open ocean shark tank, and a 4D movie theatre (worth the extra money!). The website can be foundhere, it has everything you would want to see and more.

Fort McHenry And Inner Harbor

Fort McHenry is at the end of Locust point and is the inspiriation to the song you've probably heard, the Star-Spangled Banner. When the Britishwere invading Baltimore in 1812, the song came to light due to Francis Scott Key and his experience. They have 1800s like demonstrations, tours, and events at the fort. The website can be foundhere, it is a must see!

We also love Baltimore Inner Harbor, this is the walk around and near the harbor that has events, scenic waterfront views, and activities lining the harbor. Let us know if you have any questions on what to see and do!

Fells, Canton, and DC

Breweries and north harbor* Another great location to check out is Fells point and Canton. They are both east of the inner harbor and offer waterfront views, a plethora of great food and bar options, as well as a great selection of breweries. Check out some of the things to do here. As always, let us know if you have any questions!

If you are feeling froggy and want to make the trip, we are a hop skip away from the nations capital. It can be about 40-60 minutes to drive and train into the District, where our nations history and most prominent buildings are located. Find a quick list of things to do here. Again, let us know if you have any questions!
